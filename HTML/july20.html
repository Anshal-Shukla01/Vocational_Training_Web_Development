There are only three naturally occurring species of peafowl (the Indian, Green, and Congo), but there are over 200 recognized color mutations and varieties bred today. In India and globally, the most famous variations range from the iconic iridescent blues and greens to rare colors like white, purple, bronze, and peach.The incredible variety of colors and patterns are divided by breeders into two main categories: base colors (the actual pigment) and pattern mutations (how the colors are distributed).Main Species & Natural ColorsIndian Blue: The most common variety, featuring a vibrant metallic-blue neck and a spectacular train of green, bronze, and gold eyespots.Green: Features shimmering metallic emerald-green and gold feathers on both the male and female.White: Completely snow-white from head to toe, caused by a genetic mutation known as leucism rather than true albinism.Common Color MutationsBlack Shouldered: Males feature striking black wings with patches of metallic blue, while females are mostly white with brown markings.Cameo: A brown-toned variation where the male is a soft chocolate brown and the eyespots appear in shades of tan.Silver Pied: A mix of white feathers and colored splashes, primarily in silver and dark tones.Rare Color VariationsDedicated breeders have developed deep and exotic color varieties, including:Purple: Feathers that cast a deep, shimmering violet in the sunlight.Bronze: Rich brown to copper hues.Peach: Soft, warm, pastel-orange tones.
